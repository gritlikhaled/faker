I'm Mark Zuckerberg.
I live in Palo Alto, California,
where I shape the future of digital connection.

I've always had a passion for building and creating. When I was 12, I developed "ZuckNet," a simple messaging system that allowed my family to communicate from different parts of the house. It was just the beginning of my journey into coding and entrepreneurship.

In high school, I created early versions of what would become social platforms, from simple games to a music recommendation tool called "Synapse." But it wasn't until I got to Harvard that I stumbled upon the idea that would change everything. With just a few lines of code and a small project initially intended for fellow students, I launched "The Facebook" from my dorm room—a platform to bring people together online in ways they had never experienced before.

The world opened up. By 20, I dropped out of Harvard to fully dedicate myself to Facebook, and within a few short years, it grew into the most influential social network in history, redefining how people stay in touch, share ideas, and build communities.

Today, I'm the founder of Planetaria, where we're working on civilian space suits and manned shuttle kits you can assemble at home so that the next generation of kids really can make it to orbit—from the comfort of their own backyards.

Projects

WhatsApp

WhatsApp is a messaging app launched in 2009 by Brian Acton and Jan Koum. It allows users to send text messages, voice messages, make voice and video calls, and share images and documents over the internet. Known for its end-to-end encryption, WhatsApp prioritizes user privacy. It has grown to become one of the most popular messaging platforms globally, with over two billion users, and is owned by Facebook, Inc.

Facebook

Facebook is a social media platform founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg. It allows users to connect, share content, and engage with friends and communities. With features like posts, messages, groups, and events, it has become a key tool for personal communication and business marketing, also owning Instagram and WhatsApp.

Instagram

Instagram is a photo and video-sharing platform launched in 2010. It allows users to post visual content, follow others, and engage through likes and comments. Known for its emphasis on aesthetics, it features stories, reels, and IGTV. Acquired by Facebook in 2012, it is a key platform for influencers and brands.
